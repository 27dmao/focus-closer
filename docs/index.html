The model learns your taste, then applies it forever.

Three layers stack on every classification. The bottom two compound from your keyboard feedback — you don't have to repeat yourself.

Layer 1

Hard rules

Shorts always close. Channels you've explicitly whitelisted/blocklisted resolve instantly. Domains in your blocklist (Instagram, X, etc.) close before the page renders. Zero LLM cost.

Layer 2

Personal policy

5–12 imperative rules distilled from your flag history (e.g., "Close UFC, MMA, and combat-sports highlights"). Generated by Claude Sonnet from your last 200 corrections. Travels with every classification.

Layer 3

Claude Haiku 4.5

For everything the rules + policy don't cover. ~500ms, ~$0.001/call. Strict-leaning prompt: burden of proof on the productive side. Low-confidence productive verdicts auto-flip to close.

Three ways to teach the model.

Every signal flows into the same feedback history that drives the reflection. You can correct individual decisions, bulk-train in plain English, or just press a key while you browse.

MechanismWhat it does
⌘+Shift+X Flags the current tab as distracting. Closes it. For YouTube: adds the channel to blocklist + records the title for the next reflection. For other domains: adds the hostname to blocklist.
⌘+Shift+S Flags the current tab as productive. If you just had something close, it undoes the close (within 5 min) and whitelists the video + channel.
Train by description Plain English on the Rules tab: "I scroll Instagram, MrBeast videos suck me in, I check email compulsively, I get sucked into chess highlights." Claude parses it into domains, channels, and policy rules.
Refute (Log tab) Per-row button on every classification in the log. "I disagree with this verdict" — flips the policy going forward. Refuted rows show a green pill and persist across reloads.

Onboarding probes you for a head start.

First-run is one screen with a guided probe. Skip everything if you want — the system learns from your keyboard either way. Answer what you can and the AI compresses your answers into starting rules.

Q1

Which websites distract you most?

Bare names work — Claude maps "Twitter" to x.com + twitter.com automatically.

Q2

What kinds of YouTube videos suck you in?

Channels, topics, formats, vibes. The parser captures shape, not just specific creators.

Q3

What kinds of YouTube content IS productive?

Drives the channel whitelist + "keep" policy rules. Equally important as the negative signals.

Email and core work tools (Gmail, Calendar, Slack, GitHub, AI assistants) are hardcode-protected from accidental blocks — you can't lock yourself out by accident.

Features

★

Personal policy reflection

Sonnet 4.6 reads your last 200 flags and compresses your taste into 5–12 imperative rules every 5 new corrections. Haiku fallback if Sonnet is unreachable.

⚡

Claude Haiku 4.5 classifier

Per-video. Reads title, channel, description, tags. Prompt-cached. Personal policy + recent feedback baked into every call.

↻

5-second recovery popup

False positives aren't punishing. Reopen restores the tab + teaches the override. ESC dismisses. Hover pauses the timer.

🔍

Editable system prompt

See exactly what Claude reads on every classification. Customize freely. Reset to default anytime. Full transparency, full control.

🛠

Train by description

Plain English in, structured rules out. Claude parses your description into blocklist domains, channel-block entries, and policy rules.

⌘

Keyboard-native

⌘+Shift+X flag distracting · ⌘+Shift+S flag productive. Every press updates channel lists AND feeds reflection.

$

Cost dashboard

Live spend vs. budget donut. Per-model projections (Haiku/Sonnet/Opus). Switch the classifier model from the dashboard.

📊

Attention score + heatmap

Single number trended over time. 7×24 heatmap of when you get distracted. Source breakdown for every close.

⚖

Refute any decision

Per-row button on every entry in the log. Flip the policy going forward. Refuted rows show a green pill and persist across reloads.

Convergence

After ~30 corrections spanning your distraction shapes, the policy + channel rules + Shorts handle the vast majority of your traffic. Claude calls become rare and high-confidence.

~$1.50/month
at typical use after the model converges. Most closes hit the channel/policy fast path. Claude only fires for genuinely-novel content.

Install

Five minutes. No build step. No server. Pure vanilla JS + Manifest V3.

1
Clone the repo
git clone https://github.com/27dmao/focus-closer
2
Load unpacked in Chrome
Open chrome://extensions → enable Developer mode → Load unpacked → select the repo folder.
3
Onboarding asks you 4 quick probing questions + your Anthropic API key (get one at console.anthropic.com; new accounts need a one-time credit purchase).
4
Start browsing. When something gets misclassified, hit ⌘+Shift+X or ⌘+Shift+S. Within ~30 corrections the model converges to your taste.
